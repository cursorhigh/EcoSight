Process

The "Satellite Image Classification" project began with data acquisition from Amazon's satellite imagery resources. The collected images were preprocessed to ensure uniformity and compatibility. Subsequently, a suitable deep learning model was selected and customized to the specific classification tasks. The model underwent rigorous training and validation stages, with hyperparameter tuning and transfer learning as part of the refinement process. The final evaluation demonstrated the model's effectiveness in accurately classifying objects and features within the satellite images, meeting the project's objectives.Or more step by step:

  • We started by downloading data from kaggle, here
  • After loading it we started exploring it and it contained about 40,000 images! all of the images were classified on multiple tags
  • We started by counting clasifications and found out most of the images were from "primary" category, we also made visualizations for that.
  • We added multiple columns to the labels, and basically created binary classifications for each tag, 0 if they are not present and else 1.
  • Then we used a ResNet50 model as a base model, and used a few more layers to train our overall model
  • We did some basic evaluation too, you can check everything here
